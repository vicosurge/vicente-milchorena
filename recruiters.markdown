# Notes for Recruiters

Thanks for your interest. To save us both time, please read this before reaching out. Almost every initial conversation covers the same points, so I've put them here up front. None of this is negotiable framing; it's just the reality of how I work.

---

## Work authorization

I am a **Mexican and Spanish citizen**. I do **not** hold US citizenship or a US work visa, and I am not pursuing one.

This means I can take on **remote roles** for companies anywhere, including US companies, as a remote worker or contractor. I cannot take roles that require US work authorization, onsite presence in the US, or visa sponsorship as a path to relocation. If the role depends on any of those, I'm not the right fit, and that's fine.

---

## Location & work model

I am based in **Tijuana, Baja California, México**, and I work **remote only**.

I am **not relocating**, and I will not consider hybrid or onsite roles outside of Tijuana. Please don't ask me to reconsider this; it's a settled decision, not an opening offer.

---

## Compensation

I bill in **USD at international rates**, not local Mexican rates. Being based in Mexico is not a discount. Please don't open with a low-ball; it ends the conversation faster than it starts it.

- **Full-time:** USD $40/hour and above.
- **Part-time:** negotiable.

**On currency risk:** as a contractor paid in USD, I carry exchange-rate volatility that you don't. I'm upfront about this: if the USD weakens materially against the peso, I will renegotiate the rate or end the engagement. I take my work seriously and expect the arrangement to be sustainable for both sides, so let's set a number that survives currency swings rather than one that quietly erodes.

---

## How to contact me

The fastest path is **email** with the **salary range stated in your first message**:
[vicente.munoz@milchorena.com](mailto:vicente.munoz@milchorena.com)

A few things that will keep things moving:

- **Lead with the range.** I don't share my WhatsApp, phone number, or any details beyond what's already on my CV until I know the compensation range. If the range works, we talk; if it doesn't, no time is wasted.
- **No calls before the range.** I won't schedule a call or jump on a screening just to find out the budget at the end.
- **Everything you need is on the CV.** My experience, skills, and certifications are public; [see my CV](/). Ask me about the role; I'll ask you about the number.

I'm genuinely glad to talk to recruiters who respect these terms. If the role is remote, pays USD at a fair rate, and you can tell me the range up front, then reach out and let's get to the interesting part.
